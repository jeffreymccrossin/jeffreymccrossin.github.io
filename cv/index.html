Professional Summary

Clinician-researcher who blends clinical social work, couple and family therapy, and implementation science to improve services for families of neurodiverse children. My work is rooted in equity, Indigenous partnership, and caregiver peer support across hospital, community, and private practice settings.

Highlights

  • Clinician-researcher specializing in family-centred, equity-oriented mental health care for neurodiverse children and their caregivers.

  • Leads community-engaged research with Indigenous and diverse families to adapt caregiver peer support models and implementation strategies.
  • Secured over $740,000 in peer-reviewed funding (SSHRC, FRQS, KBHN, and McMaster fellowships) while serving as a primary investigator and funded trainee.
